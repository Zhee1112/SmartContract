# ABSTRAK

Penelitian ini mengkaji optimalisasi biaya gas dan keamanan smart contract bridge pada arsitektur empat tingkatan (4-tier) berbasis EIP-1153 transient storage. Lonjakan transaksi lintas rantai dalam beberapa tahun terakhir menuntut solusi bridge yang tidak hanya hemat gas tetapi juga tangguh terhadap serangan seperti reentrancy dan MEV sandwich. Penelitian ini merancang empat arsitektur bridge—Tier A (tanpa optimasi), Tier B (optimasi statis), Tier C (pertahanan dinamis eksternal), dan Tier D (pertahanan dinamis inline)—serta membandingkannya melalui pengukuran empiris menggunakan Foundry dengan 200 sampel per operasi. Pengujian keamanan mencakup delapan fitur kritis, sementara analisis statistik menggunakan Welch's t-test, ukuran efek Cohen's d, dan interval kepercayaan 95%. Hasil menunjukkan bahwa arsitektur inline (Tier D) mencapai skor keamanan sempurna (8/8) dengan biaya gas 72-88% lebih rendah dibandingkan Tier C. Modifikasi EIP-1153 yang diusulkan bertransformasi dari sekadar reentrancy guard menjadi platform keamanan multifungsi yang mencakup lima mekanisme pertahanan. Sistem peringatan dini (Early Warning System) on-chain terbukti efektif mendeteksi pola sandwich attack dengan probabilitas 96%. Temuan ini membuktikan bahwa pendekatan inline menawarkan keseimbangan optimal antara efisiensi gas dan ketangguhan keamanan pada infrastruktur bridge lintas rantai.

**Kata kunci:** bridge blockchain; EIP-1153; keamanan smart contract; optimasi gas; transient storage

# ABSTRACT

This study investigates the optimization of gas costs and the security of smart contract bridges on a four-tier EIP-1153 transient storage architecture. The rapid growth of cross-chain transactions in recent years demands bridge solutions that are not only gas-efficient but also resilient against attacks such as reentrancy and MEV sandwich. Four bridge architectures are designed: Tier A (no optimization), Tier B (static optimization), Tier C (external dynamic defense), and Tier D (inline dynamic defense). These are compared through empirical measurements using Foundry with 200 samples per operation. Security testing covers eight critical features, while statistical analysis employs Welch's t-test, Cohen's d effect size, and 95% confidence intervals. Results indicate that the inline architecture (Tier D) achieves a perfect security score (8/8) with 72–88% lower gas costs compared to Tier C. The proposed EIP-1153 modification transforms it from a single reentrancy guard into a multifunctional security platform encompassing five defense mechanisms. The on-chain Early Warning System (EWS) proves effective in detecting sandwich attack patterns with a 96% probability. These findings demonstrate that the inline approach offers an optimal balance between gas efficiency and security robustness in cross-chain bridge infrastructure.

**Keywords:** bridge blockchain; EIP-1153; gas optimization; smart contract security; transient storage
